# The founder-discovery tool stack in April 2026

**The seven-layer pyramid you drew matches a real and rapidly consolidating market, but no single tool covers more than two adjacent layers today.** The English-speaking world has ~60 credible tools across the stack, most clustered at the extremes (Layer 1 signal capture and Layer 7 code generation), with a thin, fragmented middle. The Chinese ecosystem is 12–18 months behind on founder-discovery tooling specifically, even while it leads on content-signal mining (小红书/抖音) and has fully caught up on code agents. **The arbitrage opportunity for a ByteDance engineer with a 小红书 personal brand is almost exactly the "Layer 2–5 AI opportunity synthesizer" layer, localized to 知乎/小红书/豆瓣/B站 signals** — and it is wide open in both languages, not just Chinese. The biggest single event in this market was **GummySearch shutting down on November 30, 2025**, which vacated the category leader position in community-pain mining and sent its ~140k users fragmenting across 8+ successors with no clear winner yet.

Below is a full map of the 7 layers, the specific tools that matter, revenue/traffic signals where public, and — most importantly — where the gaps are that a solo builder with your profile could realistically fill.

## Layer 1 — raw signal capture is commoditized and saturated

Signal capture splits into five streams (search demand, community pain, commerce traces, public conversations, macro/VC/patents) and each stream now has 4–10 viable tools. The category is **saturated at the infrastructure level and fragmenting at the application level**. For SEO-style keyword demand, **Ahrefs** (~$149M 2024 revenue, 54k+ customers) and **Semrush** ($105M Q1 2025, 117k paying customers, NYSE-listed) dominate, with **DataForSEO** winning indie developers through pay-per-request APIs at roughly a 70–90% discount. **Exploding Topics** was acquired by Semrush in August 2024 at roughly $3.2M ARR and now charges $39/$99/$249/month; its claimed 87% trend-forecast accuracy still comes without any LLM "pain verb" extraction — meaning Semrush owns the trend signal but hasn't productized it for founders. **Glimpse** ($49–99/month), **AnswerThePublic** (Neil Patel-owned, $5/$49/$99), and **AlsoAsked** round out the long tail.

Community pain mining is the most interesting stream because of **GummySearch's shutdown on November 30, 2025** — triggered by Reddit's commercial Data API pricing of ~$0.24 per 1k calls, which made the ~$35k MRR/140k-user business structurally uneconomic. The successors are fragmenting instead of consolidating: **PainHunt** is the most aggressive cross-platform aggregator, scraping Reddit, HN, Twitter, App Store, Product Hunt, Dev.to, AppSumo, and Chrome Web Store with explicit "I would pay" / "budget" / "urgent" verb extraction and a 0–10 opportunity score across 90k+ indexed posts; **Reddily** uses Gemini for deep per-thread analysis on a pay-per-credit model; **SubredditSignals** and **Redreach** pivot toward lead generation rather than discovery; **F5Bot** remains free and deservedly popular as a nine-year-old keyword-alert service; and **Brand24** ($149–499/month) covers 25+ channels but only shallowly. Enterprise social listening from **Brand24**, **Awario**, and **Mention** is technically adjacent but positioned for marketing teams, not founders. **The key observation: no winner has emerged six months after GummySearch's shutdown.**

For commerce traces, **Sensor Tower** (which absorbed data.ai/App Annie in 2023) and **Apptopia** dominate but charge $30–100k/year at enterprise tier; **Appbot** ($49–159/month), **AppFollow**, **AppTweak**, and YC-backed **Anecdote** offer LLM-based review sentiment, but they're all **positioned for existing app teams' retention/support workflows, not for new-founder opportunity mining**. This is a critical gap: a tool that says "here are the three unmet jobs-to-be-done inside the 1-star reviews of the top 20 period-tracking apps, ranked by estimated willingness-to-pay" simply does not exist in productized form. Apple itself shipped LLM review summarization inside iOS 18.4 (2025), which will slowly commoditize 3rd-party sentiment — but not the founder-discovery framing.

Public conversation mining is thinner than you'd expect. **Algolia HN Search** provides free full-text indexing of ~25M+ HN items (~30M searches/month) but no relevance or entity-extraction layer on top; **Syften** tracks keywords across 10+ platforms for $15–99/month. The macro layer — **Crunchbase** ($49–199/month), **PitchBook** ($12–70k/user/year), **Dealroom** (€12,500/year), **CB Insights**, and AI-first **Harmonic.ai** (which tracks 20M companies and 150M people to surface stealth founders 6–12 months early) — is explicitly built for VCs, not founders. **The gap is obvious**: no tool combines macro VC signal + community pain + commerce complaint into one founder-facing view.

## Layer 2 — LLM extraction is where the real fight is happening

**Layer 2 is where the category-defining product will be built over the next 18 months**, and it is the narrowest layer in the stack today. The tools that genuinely integrate raw capture with structured LLM extraction of (audience, pain verb, WTP signal, competitor weakness, budget mention) are: **PainHunt** (8 streams, the most complete), **Painpoint.space** (Reddit-only with 0-100 severity scores), **Reddily** (Gemini thread analysis), **Brand24 AI** (25+ channels, enterprise priced), **Appbot** (multi-store review NLP), **CatchIntent**, and **Prems**. **Complaintminer** and a long tail of n8n-based DIY pipelines round out the indie end.

The pattern is telling: **every Layer-2 tool is single-stream or shallow-multi-stream**. PainHunt is the only one covering 3+ platforms with WTP-verb extraction, and it's early-stage with opaque pricing. **No tool cross-references a Reddit complaint against a VC-funded startup working on the same problem, against a 1-star App Store review about an incumbent's failure mode, against a Google Trends spike.** That synthesis is exactly what a founder needs and exactly what doesn't exist. BuildPad, Idea Browser, and several 2025 YC companies are circling the problem but none have reached product-market fit signal (>$1M ARR disclosed).

## Layers 3-5 — scoring, prioritization, and hypothesis structuring are almost entirely manual

These three layers are the **connective tissue of the stack and the most under-built**. Layer 3 (candidate aggregation and deduplication) is almost entirely DIY today — founders use Notion, Airtable, Coda, or the free tier of Dovetail to collect signals. Layer 4 (opportunity scoring: market size × WTP × competition × personal fit) is even thinner: there are spreadsheet templates from Lenny Rachitsky and Justin Welsh, and a handful of early tools like **IdeaSorter**, **BuildPad**, and **Ideabrowser** that attempt opportunity-score rankings, but none has crossed the threshold into a category leader. Layer 5 (turning a candidate into a sharp, testable hypothesis — target user, pain, value prop, MVP scope) is served by **PRD-generation tools** like **ChatPRD** and **Writer PRD**, and by a few Lenny's-List-class Notion templates, but the core work still happens in your own head with a blank Google Doc.

**The closest thing to a full Layer 3–5 product is enterprise VoC (Voice-of-Customer) software**: **Dovetail**, **Enterpret**, **Unwrap.ai**, **Viable**, **Kraftful**, **Monterey AI**, and **Productboard**. These are genuinely impressive on AI extraction, thematic clustering, and hypothesis structuring — Enterpret and Unwrap.ai both cross the $10M+ ARR mark — but they all start at $500/month+ and are built for product teams at Series B+ companies with 10k+ inbound customer signals per month. **A founder with zero customers cannot use any of them.** This is the single largest product gap in the entire stack: an indie-priced, solo-founder-facing tool that takes 100 raw signals from Layers 1–2 and outputs 5 ranked, hypothesis-formatted opportunity cards. Kraftful is the closest and still prices out most pre-revenue founders.

The conversation/feedback tools that span Layers 1–4 — **Grain**, **Fathom**, **Otter**, **Fireflies**, **Gong**, **Chorus**, **Read.ai** — are transcription-first and only lightly do extraction; **Canny**, **Savio**, **UserVoice**, and **Olvy** aggregate feedback but don't synthesize hypotheses. For a solo founder running 10 Mom-Test interviews, Fathom (free tier) or Otter ($10/month) + manual tagging in Notion remains the real workflow.

## Layer 6 — validation is the most exciting layer, and voice AI has crossed the threshold

**Voice-AI interview agents are now good enough to run real Mom Test interviews in April 2026, and this is the single biggest unlock of the year.** **Vapi** charges $0.05/minute for orchestration, which stacks up to a realistic $0.15–0.33/minute once you add Deepgram for STT, GPT-4o or Claude for reasoning, and ElevenLabs for TTS — meaning a 30-minute customer interview costs about $5–10 in API fees, roughly 1/20th of a human moderator. Latency is 500–800ms, which Reddit and Vapi Discord reports confirm is "natural enough" for interview work though still "weird" for customer-service calls. **Retell AI** competes at a cleaner $0.07/min with less configuration. **Bland AI**, **Synthflow**, and **ElevenLabs Conversational** round out the infrastructure layer.

On top of that infrastructure, a small cohort of **AI-moderated user-research platforms** has emerged and is growing fast. **Outset.ai** (YC, $21M raised including a $17M Series A in June 2025; clients include Microsoft Copilot, Nestlé, WeightWatchers) supports video/voice/text with screen-share for usability testing in 40+ languages. **Listen Labs** (used by Microsoft for the 50th-anniversary campaign) advertises fraud detection that cuts invalid responses from ~20% to near zero, plus auto-generated decks and persona videos. **Strella** launched an MCP connector in March 2026 making research queryable from Claude, Cursor, and VS Code. All three are US-only and enterprise-priced; European alternatives **Conveo**, **Bolt Insight**, **Tellet**, **Glaut**, and **Yasna** (€150/month transparent tier) are smaller but indie-affordable. **For your use case, a Vapi + Claude prompt + custom screener is viable today and probably 10x cheaper than Outset.**

**Synthetic Users** (syntheticusers.com) deserves special scrutiny because it's the controversial one. It prices at **~$2–27 per synthetic interview plus $5 for RAG context, runs in 1–2 minutes, and claims 85–92% parity with organic interviews** across thematic overlap, depth, and qualitative alignment — measured against ~30k interview sessions and cited by Gartner in a mid-2025 report. Nielsen Norman Group is publicly skeptical, calling it "AI-generated 'research'" and arguing it can't replace real users. A detailed test by researcher Niloufar Salehi against a real Oakland Unified School District study found the synthetic interviews plausible but generalist, missing the specific institutional pain points real families articulated. **The honest synthesis: Synthetic Users is genuinely useful as a discovery co-pilot to pre-pilot interview guides and surface obvious hypotheses in minutes, but it is not a substitute for Mom Test interviews with real target users.** Treat it like a fast, cheap sanity-check, not a validated signal.

Landing-page and ad smoke-testing is the most saturated sub-layer. **Framer** (~100M visits/month, strong AI generation), **Carrd** ($19/year, solo-founder cult favorite), **Typedream**, **Unicorn Platform**, **Durable**, **Unbounce**, **Instapage**, and **Leadpages** cover the classic tier. The AI generators have exploded: **v0** (Vercel), **Lovable** ($400M ARR as of Feb 2026 — the fastest-growing SaaS in history by ARR velocity), **Bolt.new** ($40M ARR within six months), **Relume**, **Dora**, **Popsy**, and **Framer AI**. Ad automation layers on top via **AdCreative.ai**, **Smartly.io**, and **Revealbot**. **The gap here is not "another landing-page builder" — it's a closed-loop tool that takes a hypothesis from Layer 5, auto-generates the LP, runs Meta/Google ads at a fixed budget, conducts Vapi-based interviews with the opt-ins, and outputs a kill-or-proceed recommendation.** Nobody is doing this end-to-end in a single tool. The benchmark is "20–30% waitlist signup on cold traffic means genuine demand," which is well-known to indie hackers but not automated anywhere.

Community smoke-post scheduling (Typefully, Buffer, Hypefury, Postiz, Publer) is commoditized and uninteresting as a build target; **BetaList** and **Product Hunt Ship** remain the standard community-smoke channels.

## Layer 7 — code agents are the most mature and most crowded layer

**Layer 7 is effectively won by a four-way oligopoly** — Cursor, Claude Code, Lovable, and Bolt.new — with aggressive Chinese entrants that matter specifically for your context. **Lovable hit $400M ARR in February 2026**, up from $100M in July 2025 and $200M at the Slush conference in November 2025; it raised a $330M Series B at a $6.6B valuation in December 2025 and now has ~8M users, 146 employees, and 200k+ projects created per day. CEO Anton Osika's claim that Lovable "reached $100M ARR faster than OpenAI, Cursor, Wiz, and every software company in history" is technically defensible. **Cursor (Anysphere)** raised $2.3B at a $29.3B valuation in November 2025. **Bolt.new** (StackBlitz) hit $40M ARR within six months. **Replit** reached a $3B valuation in September 2025. **Vercel's v0** closed $300M at $9.3B. Combined vibe-coding-category valuation now exceeds $48B.

**For your specific context — iOS Swift consumer apps for the Chinese market with validated hypotheses as input — the practical ranking is:** Cursor for deep Swift/SwiftUI work (broadest ecosystem, best Xcode integration via external editing), Claude Code for agentic multi-file refactors, Lovable for web LP/marketing-site generation, and Bolt.new for instant prototypes. Lovable and Bolt primarily target web/React/TypeScript and are weaker for native iOS. All four accept freeform prompts as input; none has native support for structured PRD ingestion, which remains a white-space for a thin wrapper.

Chinese code agents have fully caught up on capability and are **structurally more important to you than the English tools**: **通义灵码** (Alibaba, 200+ languages, deep Aliyun SDK optimization, strong企业私域 knowledge integration), **豆包 MarsCode** (your own company's product, free, with proprietary 豆包 model plus Claude 3.5 and GPT-4o access via Trae), **Trae** (ByteDance Singapore, fully Chinese-localized VS Code fork, free access to Claude Sonnet), **文心快码 Comate** (Baidu, strongest in CodeDebug and NL2Code), **腾讯云 AI 代码助手 / CodeBuddy** (Tencent, four-tier pricing from ¥899/year, 100% localized data), **CodeGeeX** (Zhipu, open-source-leaning), and **Meituan CatPaw** (launched November 2025, free during beta with 500 chat credits). For AI-native IDEs targeting the Chinese market, **Trae's native Chinese interface and free Claude access make it the default recommendation for Chinese indie devs.** The English community's rule that "Cursor is the best paid tool and Claude Code is the best agentic tool" holds, but Trae narrows the gap to near-zero for free-tier users.

## Chinese-market tools are deep on content signals, shallow on founder discovery

The Chinese tool stack has a fascinating asymmetry. On **Layer 1 content signal mining it is best-in-world**: **5118.com** (the 哥飞-endorsed KGR and long-tail keyword tool, with free/VIP/SVIP/Pro/Flagship tiers ranging ¥629/year SVIP to enterprise API licensing, plus specific 小红书挖词 modules) dominates Baidu-side SEO. **百度指数**, **微信指数**, **站长工具 (tool.chinaz.com)**, and **爱站网** cover the classical search-demand layer. For content-platform analytics, **千瓜数据** (xiaohongshu leader, tiered individual/team/enterprise pricing, can be meaningfully used at free tier for 评论热词 analysis and 达人画像; 千瓜 > 灰豚 > 新红 > 蝉妈妈 in data depth according to practitioner benchmarks), **飞瓜数据** (Douyin), **新榜 newrank.cn** (cross-platform), **蝉妈妈** and **蝉小红** (Douyin e-commerce + Xiaohongshu), **卡思** and **灰豚数据** round out the stack. Crucially, **千瓜's 评论热词 and 舆情预警 modules already do primitive pain-word extraction on 小红书 comments** — they just aren't positioned or marketed for founders. **This is the single biggest whitespace in the Chinese market**: a founder-facing wrapper on top of 小红书/知乎/豆瓣/B站 comments that does what PainHunt does for Reddit.

App-store review mining is covered by **七麦数据 qimai.cn**, **蝉大师 chandashi.com**, **ASO114**, and **AppDuu** — all positioned for ASO teams, none for founder discovery, same failure mode as the English tools. **Indie-dev community discovery** runs through **即刻 app** (独立开发者 and AI探索站 circles are where flowith, 胃之书, 小猫补光灯, and 陌生人闹钟 were all seeded), **V2EX**, **哥飞社群** (star-paid community + 公众号), **少数派 sspai**, and **产品沉思录**. 小红书 itself has explicitly courted indie devs — its April 2025 开发者大赛 awarded ¥500k top prize, and the platform reports 50k+ active independent developers with 独立开发-related content up 146% YoY. The reported success pattern is "**Cursor + 小红书**" as the canonical two-tool stack for Chinese indie devs, exemplified by 17-year-old 冼星朗 who shipped EMOEASE (Apple Store top-5 paid) in 20 days with zero coding background, and 陈云飞 who built 小猫补光灯 in one hour and earned near ¥1M in a year.

Enterprise PM/VoC in China — **PingCode**, **Ones.com**, **用户说**, **神策用户声音**, **吐个槽** — is organized for corporate teams and structurally unusable for solo founders, same as Dovetail/Enterpret in the West. **火山引擎** (ByteDance's own cloud) provides LLM infrastructure but no founder-discovery capability.

Chinese voice AI for interviews is viable. **讯飞星火语音**, **豆包语音** (ByteDance), **Minimax audio**, and **通义千问语音** all offer Vapi-equivalent STT/LLM/TTS orchestration, and Minimax specifically has strong Chinese-language conversational quality. **A Chinese-language Vapi-equivalent for Mom Test interviews is buildable today with 豆包语音 or Minimax APIs** and probably has no direct competitor. Chinese landing-page tools (**易企秀**, **兔展**, **微搭**, **轻流**, **简道云**) target marketing teams and feel 5+ years behind Framer/Lovable; there is a real white-space for a Chinese Lovable. Chinese AI directories **ai-bot.cn**, **17yongai.com**, and **toolify中文** are the right discovery layer for new Chinese AI tools.

## Four arbitrage opportunities for a bilingual ByteDance engineer with a 小红书 brand

**The single highest-value bet is a Chinese GummySearch-equivalent positioned for the 小红书/知乎/豆瓣/B站 comment layer with LLM pain-verb extraction.** No direct competitor exists in either the Chinese or English market that specifically targets the Chinese social graph; 千瓜 does primitive sentiment on 小红书 only but is positioned for MCN agencies, not founders; PainHunt is English-only. A tool that ingests top comments under 女性健康 or 艺考 posts, extracts "I wish there was an app that..." / "我想要" / "有没有人做" / "求推荐" patterns, clusters them into opportunity cards, and surfaces willingness-to-pay signals would be immediately useful to the 50k+ independent developers on 小红书, many of whom already know your 强子 brand. Build it with Trae or Cursor, validate with 20 Vapi calls to 即刻 users, ship the MVP on a Framer landing page, launch on 即刻 and 小红书. Estimated cost: ¥5,000 in infrastructure, 6 weeks of weekends.

**The second-best bet is the Layer 3–5 indie-priced opportunity synthesizer — the gap is identical in both languages.** PainHunt covers Layer 1–2 in English; Kraftful and Enterpret cover Layer 3–5 at $500+/month; nobody covers Layer 3–5 at $20–50/month for solo founders. A "hypothesis workbench" that takes 100 raw signals, deduplicates them with embeddings, scores them on your 5 personal-fit criteria, and outputs ranked Mom-Test-ready hypothesis cards is a natural companion to any Layer 1–2 tool. The Chinese-specific version would plug into 千瓜/新榜 instead of PainHunt.

**The third bet is the closed-loop validator.** Every piece exists — Framer AI for LPs, Meta Ads API for traffic, Vapi for interviews, Claude for synthesis — but no product has glued them into a "hypothesis in, kill-or-proceed out" workflow under $100/validated hypothesis. The DIY cost today is roughly $30–50 per hypothesis ($20 Framer + $20 Meta ads + $10 Vapi calls + your time), versus ~$500/hypothesis with Outset + Unbounce + human recruiters. A glue tool at $99/validated-hypothesis that promises a 48-hour turnaround would sell itself to the YC-stage founder segment.

**The fourth bet, narrower but simpler, is a founder-focused 1-3★ App Store mining tool.** Appbot, AppFollow, and Anecdote all do LLM review sentiment but position for retention teams. A tool that says "pick a category, I'll show you the top 20 apps, mine their 1-star reviews, cluster the complaints, and rank them by 'unmet need severity × market size'" is a clean 200-line n8n workflow wrapped in a ChatGPT-style UI. Free Apple RSS feeds make the data free. Your cost: zero. This is the obvious entry point if you want to ship something this weekend.

## Conclusion: the stack is real but the middle is empty

**The seven-layer taxonomy you sketched is correct, but the market has not yet self-organized around it.** Layers 1 and 7 are oligopoly-mature; Layer 6 (voice AI interviewers) just crossed the usability threshold in 2025; Layers 2–5 are the valuable middle where roughly $2–5B of category-defining enterprise value will be built over the next 24 months. The Chinese market specifically is deep on Layer 1 content signals, caught up on Layer 7 code agents, and essentially empty on Layers 2–5 — and that is the arbitrage. The single most defensible position for someone with your profile is **a Chinese-market Layer 1+2 tool that mines 小红书/知乎/豆瓣/B站 comments for founder opportunities, priced for solo developers, launched through your 强子 brand on 即刻 and 小红书**. The second-most-defensible is the same tool in English, targeting the ex-GummySearch audience that is currently shopping for a replacement. Everything else on the stack already has a credible incumbent — but those two slots do not, and they probably won't stay empty much longer.
